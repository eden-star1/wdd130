Malawi

Malawi's Mount Mulanje is one of the most breathtaking natural landmarks in southeastern Africa. Rising to about 3,002 meters (9,849 feet) at its highest point, Sapitwa Peak, it is the tallest mountain in Malawi and the highest point in south-central Africa. Often called the “Island in the Sky,” Mount Mulanje dramatically rises from the surrounding plains and is famous for its misty forests, waterfalls, and rare plant and animal species. The mountain is also home to the Mulanje cedar (Widdringtonia whytei), Malawi's national tree, which grows naturally only on this massif. Local legends say that Sapitwa Peak is sacred and that spirits live there, adding cultural mystery to its natural beauty. Today, Mount Mulanje attracts hikers and climbers from around the world who come to explore its scenic trails, cool mountain climate, and stunning panoramic views.
